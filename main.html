Mountain Ranges Of India

Indian Himalayas

The Himalayas are the most prominent mountain range in India, stretching across the northern region. They are home to some of the world's highest peaks, including Mount Everest, Kanchenjunga, and Nanda Devi. The Himalayas offer breathtaking landscapes, snow-capped peaks, and picturesque valleys. They also hold immense spiritual and cultural importance, with several revered pilgrimage sites nestled within their foothills.

The Eastern Ghats

The Eastern Ghats

The Eastern Ghats is a mountain range located in eastern India along the eastern coast of the Indian subcontinent. It stretches from the northern Odisha state to the southern Tamil Nadu state, running parallel to the Bay of Bengal for about 1,750 kilometers (1,090 miles). The range is discontinuous and is divided into several smaller sections by rivers and gaps.
